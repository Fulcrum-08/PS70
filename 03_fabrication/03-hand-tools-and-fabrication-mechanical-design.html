Design Process
Explanation

My inspiration for this project came from a piece of art I saw a long time ago here in Boston. In that piece, two people were playing basketball, but the interesting part was how it was displayed, on a screen made entirely of flipping black and white tiles. For this project, I didn’t have the time or patience to do something so repetitive, so instead I decided to create a wave using oscillating nails.

To do this, I built a box (see images) and ran a metal dowel through it. That dowel was connected to two scotch yoke mechanisms, one on each side. This was my first real challenge, most of the sources I found didn’t show the scotch yoke with a metal dowel running straight through it. I fixed this by cutting into the yokes so they could not only move along the pin attached to the rotating disk, but also slide back and forth around the central metal dowel itself.

At the top of the box, I cut two slits, one on either side, so the top arms of each yoke could move freely. Those two yokes were connected by another metal dowel, and around that I added a third, hollow metal dowel that could spin freely. Above that, I made what I thought was a pretty clever contraption: two horizontal boards held together by a bolt and separated by spacers (see pictures). I threaded nails between the two boards. When the machine turns on, the rotating motion gets converted into an oscillating motion across the top of the box, causing the nails to wave back and forth.

The biggest issue I ran into was torque. The motor I used fit well, stayed in place, and could turn the dowel when nothing was attached. But once I added the top structure with the nails, the load became too much, the motor didn’t have enough torque to actually move the dowel and drive the whole mechanism (see video). With more time and a shorter laser cutter line, the solution would’ve been to create a gearbox to connect the motor to the sculpture and increase the torque. I didn’t have either at the time, but I still plan on doing this in the next few days to get everything working properly. Once that’s done, I think the machine will perform just how I imagined.
