Scanning Experience
To be honest, when I first saw the 3D scanner, I thought it was going to be a lot easier. When I was scanning my head, the program kept losing the orientation and then “finding” it in the wrong spot, creating a wonky scan. Additionally, when merging different scans, the merge feature half worked, but similar to the scanning process, would mix up where the different scans fit. It especially, as you can see by the scans themselves, had a hard time with my dark hair. All this led to a multitude of hours in Blender, learning the application's features and diving into a heavy post-scan cleanup to finally reach the final result. Pretty cool though!

Design Process
Text Explanation
Looking at some of the example 3D prints, I was captivated by the unconventional techniques such as using linkages to create chainmail or even cloth to create folds. For this project I experimented with two different ideas of using 3d printing in an unusual way. The first was from Walking through Providence, Rhode Island, looking for inspiration,the idea of a flag came to me; to create a flag where the staff would be made solely from the rigid PLA. However, the flag itself could bend by inserting a cloth halfway through the print in the horizontal direction. The next idea was from talking to my mom who is involved in sustainable housing; using straw to create panels. I decided to create a kind of play on that. Panels of woven straw (really just PLA strips) that could connect to each other via embedded magnets(see pictures).Lastly I printed the first prototype for my final Project; a side of the housing for the LiDAR sensor more to come on that! Thanks to the help from Alisa, my head scan came out really well so i decided to print it as well.
