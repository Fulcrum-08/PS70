Text Explanation
This stool is inspired by the infinity bench on the first floor of the Science Center. At first, I was planning to make a small-scale chair, but due to time and logistical limitations, I decided to settle for a stool instead. The footstool is made from CNC-milled wooden parts held together by two metal dowels that provide structural support (see pictures).

One of the most challenging parts of the project was getting the holes for the dowels wide enough. They came out slightly too small, but with a bit of help from a drill and a file, I was able to fix them. Once everything fit together properly, I finished the wood with vegetable oil to protect it from moisture and bring out the grain. I also used wood glue to secure the stool in place and keep the individual parts from shifting side to side.
